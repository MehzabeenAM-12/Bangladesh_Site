Beauties of East Bengal (বাংলাদেশ)

Explore the secret treaures of Bangladesh.

You'll find yourself a spot to visit in all 8 divisions of the country, from East to West. জয় বাংলা (Victory to Bengal)!

Love spas & fancy a relaxing break? Make your way to Sreemangal's beautiful Lemon Garden Resort (Sylhet division).

Syhlet, one of the country's most economically efficient divisions, known as the powerhouse of the nation, resides its infamous 'tea capital' Sreemangal, where you can indulge in natural herbs & essences.

Admire the beach life & fancy a sunset evening boat ride? Come down to Cox Bazar (Chittagong division).


Chittagong, Bangladesh's second most populous division, is home to the world's largest sea beach, a perfect destination for sunset lovers, photographers & nature admirers.

Guilty of being a shopping addict & wish to immerse yourself in a world of fashion complexes? Join us 'guilt-free' at Gulshan (Dhaka division).

Dhaka, the nation's iconic capital, is home to Gulshan, one of the country's most affluent areas, containing some of the most luxurious tailored outlets.

Thought about wanting to visit a yoghurt city? Best believe you better not miss out on Bogra City (Rajshahi division).

Imagine visiting a city that is named after what it's famous for... Yoghurt. Rajshahi,formerly known as Rampur Boalia, is known to be the country's most 'greenest city', being so environmentally efficient in comparison to other international cities.

Are you feeling adventurous enough to explore Bangladesh's fascinating wildife? Join us at Gilatala Public Zoo (Khulna Division).

Khulna, one of the western divisions of the country, has its city named as the 'city of shrimp', with 75% of shrimp extortion taking place here. Khulna, an underrated area, has the most beautfiful natural conservations & is home to beautiful native habitats.

Bangladesh is also reputable for it's historical and beautiful mosques, such as the Baitul Aman Mosque, (Barisal Division).

This beautiful holy place is also called 'Guthia Masjid of Barisal', & it's architecure will simply mesmerise you

You mustn't leave Bangladesh without paying a visit to it's fascinating historical palaces. Take a deep dive into the nations past & visit the Tajhat Palace (Rongpur).

Tajhat palace was built by a Hindu merchant named Kumar Gopal Lal Roy (1887-1955), a jeweler by profession who managed to make his fortune in Rangpur (the most northern part of the nation).

And finally if you wish further explore the history of Bangladesh, then make your way to Mymensingh Museum (Mymensingh Division).

Visiting this museum will give you an insight to rural Bengal, showcasing its previous coins & its manuscripts.
